# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Secure Smart serves professional buyers in Israel: resellers, system integrators, ISPs, technical teams and procurement-minded installers who buy networking, wireless, switching, GPON and infrastructure equipment for projects. They arrive with product knowledge, often searching by brand, SKU, category or project need. They want confidence that the supplier is serious, responsive and commercially reliable.

## Product Purpose

The site is a B2B catalogue and trade-account gateway for Secure Smart. Its job is not to behave like a dramatic landing page or a retail shop. It should help qualified professional buyers understand the supplied brands, browse opening catalogue products, search/filter by SKU or category, and request Trade access or RFQ. Success means visitors think: this is a professional distributor I can work with.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, professional. The interface should feel elegant, restrained, structured and commercially trustworthy. It should look closer to a European B2B distributor portal such as EETGroup than a cinematic startup landing page.

## Anti-references

- Dark cinematic marketing pages with oversized typography.
- Generic landing pages that feel like a single image/mockup rather than a working website.
- Invented filler content added only to fill space.
- Loud fonts, huge hero text, decorative gradients, repetitive AI-looking card grids.
- Open retail-store pricing or consumer-phone positioning.
- Huawei logo/consumer phone implication. Huawei appears only as text for GPON and network switches.

## Design Principles

1. Function before spectacle: header, search, navigation, catalogue, filters and account/RFQ flows must feel operational.
2. Quiet confidence: small, readable typography and restrained color communicate maturity better than dramatic effects.
3. Catalogue credibility: every product card needs product name, brand, SKU, relevant specs and a clear RFQ/Trade action.
4. No public price list: pricing and availability remain gated behind professional account approval.
5. Respect the buyer's expertise: use concise technical language, not generic marketing fluff.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-friendly controls. Avoid motion that distracts from procurement tasks. Maintain readable Hebrew RTL layout with intentional handling for English brand names, SKUs and technical acronyms.
